Case Study

A real enterprise deal: from cold rejection to 5 critical vulnerabilities found. Here's exactly how it happened.

Arxiis AI · Fintech · Enterprise Cybersecurity
Suryoday Small Finance Bank
CISO-level engagement · 5 critical vulnerabilities found · Deal in active discussion · 2026
Pipeline Active Enterprise · Fintech
Step 01 · Prospecting
Multi-channel CISO Outreach

Identified and reached the CISO of Suryoday Small Finance Bank, a regulated fintech with 42L+ customers and 700+ touchpoints. Executed a coordinated outreach: cold call, targeted email, and LinkedIn network engagement to secure a first conversation.

Step 02 · Objection
"We Already Have This in Place"

On the call, the CISO dismissed the pitch immediately. "We don't need it, we already have this covered." A standard SDR would have thanked them and moved on. Instead, a reframe was ready: "Most organizations feel protected, until an attacker proves otherwise." He wasn't convinced yet.

Step 03 · The Pivot
Proof Over Pitch: The Assessment Bet

Instead of pushing harder, a conditional challenge was placed: "Sir, let me run a quick assessment on your public-facing systems. If I find 3 or more critical vulnerabilities, you agree to take this conversation forward." He agreed, confident in his team's defenses.

Step 04 · The Result
5 Criticals. One 8 Years Old.

The assessment returned 5 critical vulnerabilities. The most severe: an unpatched exposure that had been sitting undetected for 8 years. When presented to the CISO and his team, a bank managing managing millions of customers' financial data, and the room shifted. The deal moved forward immediately.

🔐
Outcome: Objection Turned Into Pipeline

A flat-out CISO rejection converted into an active enterprise deal through evidence, not pressure. The "we're safe" assumption was replaced with urgency backed by data. This is how cybersecurity SaaS gets sold to regulated financial institutions.
